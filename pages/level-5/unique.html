UNIQUE – Ensure Distinct Values

The UNIQUE constraint prevents duplicate entries in a column or group of columns.

What is the UNIQUE Constraint?

The UNIQUE constraint ensures that all values in a column (or combination of columns) are different from each other.

  • Ensuring emails, usernames, or product SKUs are not repeated
  • Acts like a secondary PRIMARY KEY (but allows one per table)
  • Often used on alternate keys or natural identifiers

UNIQUE Constraint Syntax

CREATE TABLE Employees (
  EmployeeID INT PRIMARY KEY,
  Email VARCHAR(100) UNIQUE
);

Northwind Example – Unique Email

If we want to ensure that every employee has a distinct email address, we can use the UNIQUE constraint on the Email column.
