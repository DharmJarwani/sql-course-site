LEFT JOIN ‚Äì Include All from the Left Table

Use LEFT JOIN to get all rows from the first (left) table, and matching rows from the second.

When and Why to Use LEFT JOIN?

LEFT JOIN returns all rows from the left table and the matching rows from the right table. If there's no match, NULLs will appear for right table columns.

Use LEFT JOIN when:

  • You want to retain all entries from a primary table (e.g., all customers, all products)
  • You care about items *even if* they don‚Äôt have related data
  • You‚Äôre checking for missing associations (e.g., customers with no orders)

LEFT JOIN Syntax

SELECT table1.columnA, table2.columnB
FROM   table1
LEFT JOIN table2
ON table1.common_column = table2.common_column;

Example 1 ‚Äì Customers with or without Orders

FROM   Customers c
LEFT JOIN Orders o
ON c.CustomerID = o.CustomerID;

Example 2 ‚Äì Products and Their Orders (if any)

FROM   Products p

Example 3 ‚Äì Employees and Orders They Handled

FROM   Employees e
LEFT JOIN Orders o
ON e.EmployeeID = o.EmployeeID;

Example 4 ‚Äì Categories and Their Products

FROM   Categories c
LEFT JOIN Products p

Example 5 ‚Äì Suppliers and Their Products (if any)

FROM   Suppliers s
LEFT JOIN Products p
ON s.SupplierID = p.SupplierID;

üîç INNER JOIN vs LEFT JOIN ‚Äì What's the Difference?

INNER JOIN returns only the rows that have matches in both tables.

LEFT JOIN returns all rows from the left table, whether or not there's a match in the right table. Unmatched rows will show NULL values for the right table.

Use LEFT JOIN when you care about the **existence** or **absence** of related data.
