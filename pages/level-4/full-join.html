FULL JOIN – Combine Everything

FULL (OUTER) JOIN returns all rows from both tables — matched where possible, NULL otherwise.

Why & When to use FULL JOIN?

A FULL OUTER JOIN merges the results of a LEFT JOIN and a RIGHT JOIN: you get every row from both tables, plus matches where they exist. Handy for:

  • Auditing missing relationships on either side
  • Comparing two similar tables (e.g., inventories)
  • Building reports that need “everything” in one list

FULL JOIN Syntax

SELECT  A.col1, B.col2
FROM    TableA A
FULL JOIN TableB B
ON      A.key = B.key;

Example 1 – Customers & Orders (all sides)

SELECT c.CustomerID, o.OrderID
FROM   Customers c
FULL JOIN Orders o
ON     c.CustomerID = o.CustomerID;

Example 2 – Products & Order Details

SELECT p.ProductName, od.OrderID
FROM   Products p
FULL JOIN [Order Details] od
ON     p.ProductID = od.ProductID;

Example 3 – Employees & Orders handled

SELECT e.EmployeeID, o.OrderID
FROM   Employees e
FULL JOIN Orders o
ON     e.EmployeeID = o.EmployeeID;

Example 4 – Categories & Products

SELECT c.CategoryName, p.ProductName
FROM   Categories c
FULL JOIN Products p
ON     c.CategoryID = p.CategoryID;

Example 5 – Suppliers & Products

SELECT s.CompanyName, p.ProductName
FROM   Suppliers s
FULL JOIN Products p
ON     s.SupplierID = p.SupplierID;

FULL vs LEFT / RIGHT JOIN – Key Difference

LEFT JOIN keeps every row from the left table.
RIGHT JOIN keeps every row from the right table.
FULL JOIN keeps every row from both — unmatched rows on either side get NULLs.
